About Dr Bhimroa Ambedkar

Dr B R Ambedkar was born on 14 April 1891 in the Mhow Army Cantonment,Cental provinces (present-day Madhya Pradesh) to a dalith family. Dr. Bhimroa Ramji Ambedkar popularly known as Babasaheb,was an Indian jurist economist,politician and social reformer who inspired the Modern Buddhist Movement and campaigned against social discrimination against untouchables , while also supporting the rights of women and labour. He was Independent India's first law minister (1947-51) and the principal architect of the constitution of India.
Ambedkar was a prolific student, earning a law degree and various doctorates from Columbia University and the London school of Economics,and gained a reputation as a scholer for his research in law,economics and political science.In his early career he was an economist , professor,and lawyer.His later life was marked by his political activities;he became involved in campaigning negotiations for India's independence,publishing journals advocating political rights and social freedom for Dalits,and contributing significantly to the establishment of the state of India.
In later of his life,his health worsened,and he passed away on 6 December 1956 in his sleep at his home in Delhi.His birth date celebrated as 'Ambedkar jayanthi'.He was posthumously given the Bharat Ratna in 1991.
